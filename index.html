Work Philosophy
Traceability starts where production happens

EU traceability frameworks will only succeed if they account for the realities of Tier 2–4 production. I bring that missing ground-level perspective to academic research.

Production-floor traceability

Having managed sourcing across Bangladeshi garment factories, I understand where traceability data breaks down — at the raw material, spinning, and dyeing stages. My research starts from these gaps, not from theoretical frameworks alone.

Regulation-ready data

Through the DPP4CD project with MUD Jeans, I develop practical data collection protocols aligned with EU ESPR, PEF, and the upcoming Textile Labelling Regulation — translating regulatory complexity into actionable supplier guidance.

Technology-aware research

I investigate how blockchain, RFID, IoT sensors, and interoperability standards (GS1/EPCIS, JSON-LD) can create tamper-proof, machine-readable traceability records across multi-tier textile supply chains.

Global South perspective

Traceability systems often fail at the first mile. My cross-cultural fluency and network across Bangladeshi, Dutch, and Ghanaian textile ecosystems enables inclusive research that works for suppliers, not just brands.

Current Research — Applied LCA & DPP

DPP4CD: Digital Product Passports for Circular Denim

Operationalizing LCA data collection for EU ESPR compliance

Collaborating with MUD Jeans and Tejidos Royo (Valencia, Spain) to develop standardized data collection and validation procedures for Digital Product Passports across the circular denim supply chain (Tier 2–4). Defining system boundaries, functional units, and environmental impact categories aligned with PEF methodology. Addressing critical data gaps using secondary proxies from bAwear, Ecochain, and GRS/OCS certifications.

Deliverables: LCA data protocol, PEF-aligned environmental indicators, data gap analysis with responsible proxy strategies, step-by-step DPP guidance for circular denim

Project 1 — Market Exploration & Circular SCM

Kantamanto Social Club

Circular supply chain strategy for remanufactured textiles

Mapped the complete circular supply chain for remanufactured garments from discarded home textiles in the Netherlands. Conducted B2B (31) and B2C (186) surveys assessing traceability expectations, pricing, and certification concerns. Identified supply chain partners (Kringcoop, DCW, Makers Unite) and developed a traceable circular business model from waste collection through sorting, remanufacturing, and retail.

1 kg remanufactured cellulosic textiles saves ~16.86 kg CO₂; 93% of B2B respondents familiar with remanufacturing; 64% interested in integration

Project 2 — Smart Textile Prototyping

Noosi: Responsive Cushion for Dementia Care

IoT sensor integration and material traceability in smart textiles

Developed a responsive smart cushion with integrated piezoresistive and capacitive sensors for detecting anxiety in dementia patients. Relevant to traceability: established material provenance documentation for all textile components (Bouclé PES cover, Twill PES inner layer, Silver-tech conductive yarns), demonstrating how IoT-enabled textiles require traceable material and component data for certification and regulatory compliance.

Bouclé PES: zero yarn breakage to 20,000 cycles. Arduino-based sensing with full material traceability documentation

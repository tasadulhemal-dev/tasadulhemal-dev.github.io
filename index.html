02 — Featured case study

Digital Product Passport for Circular Denim

In collaboration with MUD Jeans (NL) Tejidos Royo (Valencia, ES)

A Digital Product Passport framework built for a real circular-denim supply chain. The work spans three tiers — yarn, fabric, and dyeing & finishing — and translates EU regulatory direction (ESPR, CSDDD) into operational data flows that suppliers can actually fill in.

On the methodology side, I co-developed a PEFCR v3.1-aligned yarn-mapping diagram in swim-lane format, designed blank data-collection matrices to gather Scope 3 / LCI data from Tier 2/3 suppliers, and authored three Standard Operating Procedures defining how DPP attributes hand off between supplier tiers. Final discussion and recommendations were delivered directly to industry partners.

Methodology

  • PEFCR v3.1-aligned yarn mapping (swim-lane visualisation)
  • Multi-tier supplier data flows, Tier 2 / Tier 3 coverage
  • Scope 3 / LCI data-collection matrices
  • GS1/EPCIS-style attribute hand-offs between tiers

Deliverables

  • 3× A4 Standard Operating Procedures (Yarn; Fabric; Dyeing & Finishing)
  • Swim-lane DPP architecture diagram
  • Blank data-collection matrix (Word + PDF)
  • Discussion, conclusion, recommendations deck for industry partners
  • Stakeholder correspondence with Tejidos Royo

Team

  • Three-person student team under Saxion supervisor Laure Erkens
  • Direct collaboration with MUD Jeans & Tejidos Royo

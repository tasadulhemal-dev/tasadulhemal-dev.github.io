Projects
Selected work at Saxion ITD

Three projects showcasing my range across market research, smart textile prototyping, and sustainability data frameworks.

Project 1 — Market exploration

Kantamanto Social Club

Strategy for Ghanaian-inspired remanufactured garments

Led market research for Kantamanto Social Club, a community-driven enterprise transforming discarded home textiles into fashion items inspired by Ghanaian upcycling methods. Conducted B2B surveys (31 responses) and B2C surveys (186 responses) to assess market demand, pricing expectations, and consumer concerns. Developed a circular business model with a complete supply chain roadmap including sourcing from Kringcoop, manufacturing through DCW and Makers Unite, and retail through sustainability-focused channels.

Key finding: remanufacturing 1 kg of cellulosic textiles saves approximately 16.86 kg CO2 compared to recycling, while creating 1.4 to 4.3 full-time jobs monthly.

Project 2 — Prototyping

Noosi Smart Cushion

Responsive textile cushion for dementia care

Collaborated with Noosi to develop a smart cushion that detects anxiety indicators and provides calming sensory stimulation for people with advanced dementia during hoist transfers. Researched cover layer fabrics (Bouclé PES selected for comfort and durability), inner protective layers (Twill PES), and integrated piezoresistive and capacitive sensors through embroidery and knitting techniques. Programmed Arduino-based sensing and actuation (heating pad + vibration).

Applied the Double Diamond research model and MoSCoW prioritization. Bouclé PES showed no yarn breakage up to 20,000 abrasion cycles with minimal wash shrinkage.

Smart textiles Healthcare Arduino / C++ Material testing
Project 3 — Applied research

DPP4CD

Digital Product Passports for circular denim

Working with MUD Jeans and Saxion's research team to operationalize LCA data collection for Digital Product Passports under the EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR). Defining system boundaries, functional units, and environmental impact categories for circular denim. Collecting primary data from MUD Jeans' supply chain and complementing with secondary sources (bAwear, Ecochain, GRS/OCS certifications).

Expected outputs include an LCA data protocol with step-by-step guidance, processed environmental indicators aligned with Product Environmental Footprint (PEF) methodology, and analysis of data gaps with responsible proxy strategies.
